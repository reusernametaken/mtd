The short version of why this exists at all, until there's a first real post to put here: there's a stretch of Colorado the internet forgot. Grand County, Hot Sulphur Springs, Parshall, Granby, Fraser, Winter Park, over Berthoud Pass — thousands of people live, work, kayak, ranch, ski, and drive through terrain where the signal stops at the valley floor. No bars when the highway closes. No bars when the creek floods. Mesh That Divide is the fix, built in public, one node at a time.
