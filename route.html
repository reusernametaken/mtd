The Route

Mesh That Divide follows Highway 40 — the corridor that already connects these towns, and always has. Western anchor is kNET in Kremmling, already online. From there the chain climbs toward the Continental Divide and comes back down the other side:

  • Bross, 9,250ft — the crucial waypoint bridging the western anchor toward the Divide crossing, while covering Hot Sulphur Springs, Parshall, Granby, Fraser, and Winter Park along the way.
  • Parshall Divide Ridge, 8,009ft — valley coverage.
  • Granby Ranch, 8,467ft — Fraser Valley coverage.
  • Fraser / Fisher's Bar — community-hosted hub.
  • Winter Park — community-hosted repeater, gateway to Berthoud Pass.
  • Berthoud Pass, 12,426ft — the network's hub and highest point. One node, two hops: north to Fraser, east to the Front Range. The entire crossing pivots here.
  • Ute Benchmark, 10,349ft — eastern relay to the Front Range.
  • Eastern anchor: existing ColoradoMesh nodes at Evergreen, Conifer, and Golden.

Total corridor: roughly 120 miles, crossing the Continental Divide. See Node Chain Status for elevation and land-status detail on each stop, or the live map: caltopo.com/m/TD9BS47 ↗
