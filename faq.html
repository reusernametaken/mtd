FAQ

Is this legal?

Yes. Meshtastic operates on the unlicensed 915MHz ISM band. No license required.

Who owns the network?

The community. Every transaction runs through Open Collective, public by default — no individual can pocket funds. Nodes placed on public land belong to the public.

Does my radio actually strengthen the mesh, or is that just a slogan?

It's literal. Every Meshtastic device relays messages for others by default. The more people carrying radios, the stronger and more resilient the network gets. You don't just use Mesh That Divide — the moment your radio is on, you are part of it.

What about nodes on private property?

Community-hosted nodes are fully autonomous and self-powered: no liability to the host, no power draw from their structure, no modifications to anything. Removable without a trace — and don't you dare take that as permission to remove one that isn't yours.

How does this connect to ColoradoMesh?

Mesh That Divide bridges the isolated western slope to the existing ColoradoMesh Front Range infrastructure. It's being built in coordination with the ColoradoMesh community, not in competition with it.

Why Highway 40 and not I-70?

Because I-70 already has cell coverage. Highway 40 doesn't. The people who need this most live along the road that got left behind.
