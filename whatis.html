What is Meshtastic?

Meshtastic is an open-source, low-power, long-range radio protocol built on LoRa. No towers, no subscription, no signal fee. Every device that carries it -- every radio in every pocket -- participates in the network instead of just consuming it.

That's the part that makes it the right fit for this corridor, not just a novelty:

  • A backbone node on a ridgeline covers 10–20 miles of terrain.
  • A person carrying a radio in Granby extends that coverage into their own neighborhood.
  • A kayaker in Byers Canyon becomes a relay point without doing anything but being there.
  • A rancher outside Parshall strengthens the mesh around their own property just by existing on it.

The backbone -- kNET, Bross, Parshall Divide Ridge, and the rest of the chain -- creates the corridor. The community brings the signal the rest of the way, down off the ridges and into the valleys, the canyons, the places people actually live. That's not a metaphor, it's the literal architecture: you don't just use Mesh That Divide, you are Mesh That Divide, the moment you turn a radio on.

Legal, no strings

Meshtastic runs on the unlicensed 915MHz ISM band. No license required, no permission needed, no company standing between you and the waves.
