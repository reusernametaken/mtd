Node Builds

Each node is a weatherproof, solar-powered, LiFePO4-backed Meshtastic relay, built for Colorado mountain conditions — not a lab prototype pretending it'll survive a Grand County winter.

Standard Highway 40 Relay Node — $150–180

  • LoRa radio module (Heltec LoRa 32 V3 or Wio SX1262), Meshtastic firmware
  • LiFePO4 battery pack, cold-weather rated
  • 5–10W solar panel + PWM charge controller
  • 3dBi 915MHz antenna
  • Weatherproof enclosure with upcycled polystyrene insulation
  • Mounting hardware

Berthoud Pass Flagship Node — $500–600

The hub of the entire network. At 12,426ft on the Continental Divide, this node faces Colorado winters head-on — months of low solar gain, rime ice, sustained winds that would tear apart conventional equipment. It's powered by wind, because wind is the one thing Berthoud Pass never runs out of.

  • 600W wind turbine with passive aerodynamic tail furling — automatically turns the rotor out of the wind past safe operating speed, no electronics, no maintenance, gravity does the work
  • LiFePO4 battery pack, 12V 30Ah, cold-weather rated
  • Wind charge controller with dump load
  • Run/stop switch — emergency manual brake
  • 6dBi fiberglass antenna on a permanent T-post mount
  • Weatherproof enclosure, alpine-rated insulation
  • Meshtastic firmware, configured as router/repeater
